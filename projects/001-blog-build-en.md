## Sprint Overview

In **March 2026**, I did a quick **execution-focused** sprint to build a personal knowledge base in Quarto. This short post reflects on what worked, what could be improved, and how to keep momentum.

- Define the repository structure early
- Use templates to standardize notes
- Keep the first version simple and **deploy quickly**

## Key Lessons

The fastest way to get traction is to ship a minimal **working base** and iterate. I focused on:

- A clean file structure for Projects, Areas, Resources, Archives
- Consistent frontmatter for **cards and metadata**
- A quick review loop: publish, read, adjust

## What’s Next

Moving forward, I plan to maintain the **execution rhythm** with weekly check-ins and a short list of improvements. The project is now in a state where additions are easy and the site stays responsive to real needs.

- Keep a short list of action items
- Review the build process monthly
- Share the system with a peer for feedback

This sprint shows that with focused effort and **strong execution**, a Quarto site can become a reliable home for ongoing work and learning.
